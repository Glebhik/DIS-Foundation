Architecture & Governance

Technology

Durable competitive advantage requires owning the intelligence layer — not just connecting to it. DIS Group builds proprietary intelligence on top of high-quality external infrastructure.

Philosophy

Architecture as competitive strategy

DIS Group's technology architecture is the infrastructure layer that makes every business unit more intelligent than its competitors.

The architecture must enable AI capabilities to be deployed across all business units from a shared foundation, scale cleanly as the business grows, keep data secure and accessible, and be maintainable by a small, high-quality engineering team.

Technology choices that compromise data quality, accessibility, or security are rejected regardless of other merits. Deviations from established technology standards require a written Architecture Decision Record.

D

Data First

Every architectural decision is evaluated on its impact to data quality, accessibility, and security. Technology choices that compromise data assets are rejected.

C

Composable over Monolithic

A well-chosen set of composable tools is easier to evolve than a single all-in-one system. We prefer systems that can be combined and reconfigured.

O

Observability as First-Class

We cannot improve what we cannot see. Every system exposes metrics, logs, and traces. Monitoring is proactive, not reactive.

AI Governance

Governance-first AI deployment

DIS Group's AI governance policy is operational today. No AI system touches production data, external parties, or material business decisions without completing a Pre-Deployment Review under the four-class risk framework.

ClassDefinitionExamplesGovernance Requirement
Class 1 — Low Risk Internal productivity tools; outputs reviewed by a human before action Summarisation, document drafting, search Lightweight — log and document
Class 2 — Medium Risk Systems that inform significant decisions; outputs may be acted on without manual review Market intelligence, research synthesis Pre-Deployment Review + quarterly audit
Class 3 — High Risk Systems with direct financial, legal, or safety implications Trading decision support, contract analysis Full review process; continuous monitoring
Class 4 — Critical Systems making or directly triggering consequential real-world actions Autonomous trade execution, automated filings Board-level approval; continuous human oversight

Governance Principles

  • Human accountability — every AI system has a named human owner
  • Transparency — we know what our systems do, what data they use, and what their failure modes are
  • Proportionality — governance overhead proportionate to risk
  • Continuous monitoring — deployment begins, not ends, the governance obligation
  • Privacy and data minimisation — minimum data necessary for each use case

For DIS Travel specifically

  • Travel intelligence outputs informing purchasing: Class 2 (quarterly audit)
  • Outputs triggering automated actions: Class 3 (continuous monitoring)
  • No personal traveller data passed to external LLM APIs
  • Immutable audit log for all Class 2+ AI operations
  • AI components not shipped until Pre-Deployment Review is complete
Data Protection

GDPR as a design constraint

DIS Group is incorporated in Ireland and operates under EU law. GDPR compliance is a data model constraint — not a compliance checklist applied after the system is built.

Data minimisation and purpose limitation are enforced at the schema level. For DIS Travel specifically: traveller PII is absent from the intelligence layer because the data model does not collect it there. This eliminates a class of compliance risk rather than managing it.

EU data residency is an infrastructure-level constraint: cloud provider selection is governed by an ADR process with EU residency as a hard requirement. No personal traveller data transits outside the EU boundary.

Data TypeHandling
Traveller identity (name, passport, employee ID)Stays within customer's organisational boundary — not transmitted to intelligence layer
Trip records (routes, dates, carrier)Processed under customer DPA — used for intelligence, not shared with third parties
Carbon calculationsOwned by the reporting organisation — structured as an organisational, not individual, asset
Partner API queriesAnonymised or aggregate — no personal data transmitted to travel content partners
Decision Governance

Architecture Decision Records

Every major technology decision at DIS Group is governed by an Architecture Decision Record: a documented record of the decision drivers, options evaluated, and rationale — written before committing to a direction, not after.

Why ADRs

Technical decisions made without explicit documentation accumulate as invisible constraints. ADRs make the reasoning visible — so future decisions can build on it or deliberately revisit it rather than unknowingly violating it.

What they govern

Cloud infrastructure selection, data platform choice, integration architecture design, AI deployment decisions, and security controls. Deviations from technology standards require an ADR — governance through visibility, not hard locks.

Status in DIS Travel

Cloud provider selection for DIS Travel is currently in the ADR process — constrained to EU-resident providers. The constraint is established; the provider decision is pending. This is deliberate governance, not an open question.
