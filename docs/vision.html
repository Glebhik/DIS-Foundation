Long-Range Direction

Vision

Where DIS Group is going — and the four horizons that define how it gets there.

The Vision Statement

DIS Group will be the leading intelligent industrial group in its markets — a company where world-class physical operations and AI-native intelligence are inseparable.

By 2030, every DIS business unit is a data-generating, AI-augmented operation. Every decision is informed by better information than our competitors have. Every process is more efficient than the industry standard. The combination creates a compounding advantage that is difficult to replicate.

The Thesis

Why this vision

The next decade of value creation in industrial and energy markets will not go to companies with the most assets. It will go to companies that know more, move faster, and operate smarter.

Physical operations — recycling, energy, materials — are becoming information problems. The companies that build the intelligence layer on top of industrial operations first will set the terms for everyone else.

DIS Group is building that company.

What We Will Not Do

  • Pursue volume without intelligence — scale for its own sake destroys margins
  • Enter markets where we cannot build a data or operational advantage
  • Sacrifice ESG integrity for short-term returns
  • Build businesses that require luck to succeed — we build businesses that require knowledge
Strategic Horizons

Four phases of execution

Each horizon builds on the previous. The foundation must be solid before the intelligence layer is added. The intelligence advantage must compound before category leadership is pursued.

Horizon 1  ·  Now to 12 Months

Establish the Foundation

Build the operating system before scaling the operations. All business units running with defined processes, clear KPIs, and real-time visibility. AI infrastructure deployed. ESG baseline established.

The group operates like one company, not three independent businesses.

Horizon 2  ·  1 to 3 Years

Scale with Intelligence

Grow the businesses using the intelligence advantage. Technology and AI capabilities recognised externally, attracting talent and partners. First strategic partnerships activated in each business unit.

DIS Group is visibly different from peers — faster decisions, better data, leaner operations.

Horizon 3  ·  3 to 5 Years

Build Defensible Positions

Convert intelligence advantage into structural moats. Market leadership in chosen recycling commodities and geographies. ESG platform potentially offered as a service. Proprietary data assets that create barriers to entry.

Competitors are copying our playbook. We are already three moves ahead.

Horizon 4  ·  5 to 10 Years

Category Definition

DIS Group is the reference company for intelligent industrial operations in its markets. New business units added using the same blueprint: physical operations plus intelligence layer. Diversified, resilient cash flows across economic cycles.

When people describe how to build an intelligent industrial company, they describe DIS Group.

Operating Principles

The constraints we operate within

These principles govern how DIS Group makes decisions. When a decision conflicts with a principle, the principle wins.

1

Intelligence Before Scale

We do not grow until we understand. Clear data on what is working precedes any expansion of operations. Unintelligent scale destroys value; intelligent scale compounds it.

2

Integrity Without Exception

We do not optimise short-term optics at the expense of long-term substance. Our ESG commitments are binding, not marketing. We report accurately and assess risk honestly.

3

Systems Over Individuals

DIS Group is built to outlast any individual. Knowledge is documented. Decisions are recorded. The organisation's intelligence lives in its systems.

4

Long-Term Thinking

Capital allocation, hiring, and product decisions are evaluated on 3–5 year horizons. Short-term metrics are indicators, not objectives.

5

Simplicity as Discipline

Complexity is a cost. We prefer simple structures, clear processes, and direct communication. When something becomes hard to explain, we simplify it — we do not add process around it.
