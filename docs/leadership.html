Professional Background

Building DIS Group

Glib Vakunov founded DIS Group and has built three operating businesses across recycling, energy, and ESG data infrastructure — each grounded in the conviction that physical industrial operations become more valuable when built on intelligent software.

At DIS Group, Glib has designed the complete corporate operating system: architectural decision governance, an operational AI governance framework, ESG data infrastructure aligned to CSRD disclosure standards, and the DIS Travel architecture. Every major technology and capital decision is documented in writing before commitment — a practice that begins with the founder.

He conceived and leads the DIS Travel initiative — an AI-native B2B travel intelligence platform being built in active partnership conversations with enterprise-grade travel data providers. Architecture is complete; build is in progress.

Management Philosophy

Three operating beliefs

These are not mission-statement generalities. They are principles that govern daily operating decisions at DIS Group.

1

Institutions Over Individuals

A company built around one person's knowledge fails when that person leaves. Every important decision at DIS Group is documented. Every process has a written procedure. The organisation's intelligence lives in its systems, not its founder.

2

Honest Measurement

DIS Group does not optimise for optics. ESG targets are SMART — specific and verifiable. Development status is communicated accurately: what is designed, what is built, what is estimated. Dishonest communication of progress destroys trust and eventually destroys organisations.

3

Intelligence Before Growth

DIS Group does not scale anything it does not yet understand. Understanding is measured by data quality, process documentation, and decision accuracy — not by revenue or headcount alone. Scale without intelligence magnifies problems.

Governance-First Approach

Governance as competitive advantage

Glib designed DIS Group's governance infrastructure before scaling operations. This is not regulatory compliance — it is an operational choice.

Companies that add governance frameworks after problems emerge pay a higher cost than companies that build them as a foundation. DIS Group's Architecture Decision Records, AI governance policy, and ESG data infrastructure were operational before DIS Travel's build phase began.

For enterprise partnerships and institutional relationships, documented governance is evidence of organisational maturity — not overhead.

Governance Infrastructure in Place

  • AI governance policy — four-class risk framework, Pre-Deployment Review process
  • Architecture Decision Records — documented for all major technology choices
  • GDPR compliance — designed at the data model layer, not retrofitted
  • ESG data infrastructure — CSRD-aligned, SMART targets, methodology-cited
  • Corporate Knowledge Base — full operating system documented and maintained
AI-First Organisation

What AI-native actually means

DIS Group is built as an AI-native organisation from the foundation layer. "AI-native" is a structural commitment, not a positioning statement.

AI in the Architecture

The DIS Travel intelligence layer — policy analysis, destination briefings, carbon attribution — is AI by design. The system architecture was defined with AI components as primary, not added after.

AI in the Operations

The corporate knowledge base — strategy, operations, finance, legal — is structured for both human and AI consumption. AI assists with knowledge synthesis, cross-domain consistency, and gap detection.

AI with Governance

Every AI system at DIS Group operates under the four-class risk framework. Class 1 runs with lightweight documentation. Class 3–4 requires Pre-Deployment Review and continuous oversight. No AI system deploys without a named human owner.
