Strategic Alliances

Partnerships

DIS Group partners to access capabilities it cannot build faster or better internally. Partnerships require clear value exchange, defined scope, and a long-term commitment horizon.

Philosophy

Partners, not vendors

DIS Group's documented partnership principle: we partner to access capabilities we cannot build faster internally. Partnerships require clear value exchange, defined scope, and exit conditions.

This means DIS Group does not enter partnership discussions to "explore" in general. When we initiate a partnership conversation, it is because we have a specific, documented architectural need that the partner is uniquely positioned to meet, and because we have evaluated the build, buy, and partner options explicitly.

The Build/Buy/Partner framework is applied to every material capability decision. The decision and its rationale are recorded in an Architecture Decision Record before commitment.

B

Build

Where the capability is core to competitive differentiation and can be built to required quality within the timeline. DIS Travel's intelligence layer is built.

B

Buy

Where the capability is commodity, the market has mature solutions, and building would be a distraction. Infrastructure components, analytics tooling, and standard services are candidates.

P

Partner

Where the capability requires established data infrastructure, market coverage, or network effects that cannot be built to adequate depth. Travel content data for DIS Travel is a partner decision.

DIS Travel — Data Partnerships

Why DIS Travel engages partners before launch

A travel intelligence platform that cannot provide comprehensive route coverage, real-time pricing, and carrier metadata is not a travel intelligence platform. These capabilities require established data infrastructure that DIS Group cannot replicate to the required depth or quality.

The architectural requirement

DIS Travel's ICAO-methodology carbon calculation requires aircraft-type data at route level. This is not available from consumer-facing sources at the granularity that CSRD audit-defensible Scope 3 reporting requires. A structured data partnership is an architectural necessity — not a commercial arrangement.

Why before launch

The right time to design a data partnership is before the product architecture is constrained by production deployments. DIS Group engages with potential data partners at the architecture phase so the integration is designed correctly once — not retrofitted after launch.

What the partnership involves

Three integration layers: (1) content foundation — route, schedule, pricing, and carrier data; (2) booking distribution — referral architecture for corporate bookings; (3) carbon data enrichment — aircraft-type data for ICAO-compliant carbon attribution.
