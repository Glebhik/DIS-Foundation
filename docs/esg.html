ESG as Strategic Capability

DIS Group treats ESG not as a reporting obligation but as data infrastructure. Credible ESG creates access to capital, talent, and markets. We treat it as a strategic asset, not a compliance checkbox.

Approach

Not compliance. Infrastructure.

DIS Group's ESG platform integrates environmental, social, and governance data across all business units to drive better decisions — not just better disclosures.

The distinction matters. A compliance-oriented ESG approach produces reports. An infrastructure-oriented approach produces a data asset that improves every capital allocation, procurement, and operational decision the company makes.

DIS Group's operating principle: "Our ESG commitments are binding, not marketing." This is enforced through documented governance, not enforced through culture statements.

What best-in-class ESG infrastructure delivers

  • Access to capital markets that require verified ESG data
  • Talent attraction from candidates who evaluate ESG credentials
  • Supply chain credibility with customers who scrutinise supplier ESG
  • Regulatory readiness for CSRD, TCFD, and emerging disclosure requirements
  • Operational intelligence — carbon data that improves procurement decisions, not just reports
Three Pillars

Environmental · Social · Governance

Each pillar has its own data infrastructure, measurement methodology, and reporting standards. All three are integrated into a single platform.

  • Risk identification and tracking across business units
Regulatory Context

CSRD and the disclosure mandate

The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), in force from 2024, mandates structured, audit-defensible ESG reporting for tens of thousands of in-scope European organisations.

DIS Group has designed its ESG data infrastructure to be CSRD-compliant from the start. Environmental data cites measurement methodology and baseline year. Targets are SMART — Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Reports cite the disclosure standard used (GRI, SASB, TCFD) and are structured for external audit.

The same carbon infrastructure that underpins DIS Group's own ESG platform is the foundation for DIS Travel's Scope 3 Category 6 carbon engine — an application of the group's ESG capability to corporate travel management.

Disclosure Standards Used

  • GHG Protocol Scope 1, 2, and 3 greenhouse gas accounting and reporting standard
  • ICAO Carbon Methodology Aircraft-type emissions factors for aviation Scope 3 attribution
  • CSRD / ESRS EU Sustainability Reporting Standards — structured disclosure to regulatory standard
  • TCFD Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures
ESG Applied to Corporate Travel

Scope 3 Category 6 — Business Travel

Business travel emissions (Scope 3 Category 6) are a material and previously difficult-to-measure component of enterprise carbon footprints. CSRD makes accurate measurement a legal requirement for in-scope organisations.

The measurement problem

Expense-export-based carbon estimates — the current standard — use industry average emissions factors. They do not account for aircraft type, routing, seat class, or load factor. The resulting figures are insufficient for CSRD audit purposes.

The DIS Travel approach

DIS Travel's carbon engine uses ICAO methodology — aircraft-type emissions factors, routing, seat class, and load factor inputs — calculated at trip creation time and stored with full methodology provenance. This is the standard that CSRD audit readiness requires.

DIS Travel is an application of DIS Group's ESG data infrastructure capabilities to the corporate travel domain. The same data rigour applied to DIS Group's own ESG reporting is the foundation of the DIS Travel carbon engine.
