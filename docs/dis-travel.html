The Product

What DIS Travel is

DIS Travel gives corporate travel managers, finance directors, and sustainability officers the intelligence to manage policy compliance, destination risk, and Scope 3 carbon reporting — without replacing the booking tools they already use.

The platform integrates with an organisation's existing booking infrastructure. It does not require displacing a booking system. That is an explicit architectural choice: it removes the procurement objection that blocks every competitive booking platform.

What DIS Travel is not

  • Not a booking platform
  • Not a travel management company (TMC)
  • Not a consumer travel tool
  • Not a replacement for existing corporate booking tools
  • Not an expense management platform

DIS Travel is the intelligence layer. Booking stays where it is.

Regulatory Context

CSRD and Scope 3 Category 6

The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), in force from 2024, mandates Scope 3 Category 6 business travel emissions reporting for tens of thousands of in-scope European organisations.

The challenge for enterprise travel managers is not the obligation — it is the methodology. CSRD audit readiness requires carbon attribution at route level, with documented methodology, aircraft-type inputs, and calculation provenance. Expense-export-based estimates do not meet this standard.

DIS Travel's carbon engine is built specifically for this standard: ICAO-methodology calculation at trip-creation time, stored with full methodology provenance, structured for direct flow into CSRD reporting workflows.

Architecture

Four design decisions that matter

These are not technology preferences. They are architectural choices with specific commercial and compliance rationales.

1

Intelligence separable from booking

The intelligence layer operates independently of any specific booking system. DIS Travel integrates with an organisation's existing booking tool — it does not require replacing it. This removes the booking displacement objection from the enterprise procurement conversation.

2

Carbon as a first-class data type

Carbon attribution is calculated at trip-creation time, stored as a structured entity with full methodology provenance, and queryable at any level of aggregation. It is not a dashboard generated from expense exports. This is what makes CSRD audit readiness achievable rather than approximated.

3

GDPR as a data model constraint

Data minimisation and purpose limitation are enforced at the schema level. Traveller PII is not present in the intelligence layer because the data model does not collect it there. This eliminates a class of compliance risk rather than managing it.

4

Governance-first AI deployment

AI components are not shipped until pre-deployment review is complete, risk classification is assigned, and audit logging is confirmed. This adds deployment time. It is the correct trade-off for a platform that enterprise procurement teams will scrutinise.
