# Principles — DIS Group

These are the principles that govern how DIS Group operates. They are not aspirational statements — they are behavioral constraints. When a decision conflicts with a principle, the principle wins.

---

## Core Values

### 1. Intelligence Before Scale
We do not grow until we understand. Before expanding any operation, we require clear data on what is working, what is not, and why. Unintelligent scale destroys value; intelligent scale compounds it.

### 2. Integrity Without Exception
We do not compromise on honesty — with ourselves, our partners, or the market. This means accurate reporting, honest risk assessment, and never optimizing short-term optics at the expense of long-term substance. Our ESG commitments are binding, not marketing.

### 3. Systems Over Individuals
DIS Group is built to outlast any individual. Knowledge is documented. Decisions are recorded. Processes are written down. The organization's intelligence lives in its systems, not only in people's heads.

### 4. Long-Term Thinking
We optimize for durable value creation, not quarterly performance. Capital allocation, hiring, and product decisions are evaluated on 3-5 year horizons. Short-term metrics are indicators, not objectives.

### 5. Simplicity as Discipline
Complexity is a cost. We prefer simple structures, clear processes, and direct communication. When a system, document, or process becomes hard to explain, that is a signal to simplify — not to add more process around it.

## Business Principles

### Capital Allocation
- Capital goes where it generates the highest risk-adjusted return over the relevant horizon
- We do not allocate capital to defend legacy positions — we allocate to build future ones
- Every significant investment requires a written decision memo with explicit assumptions
- See [finance/README.md](finance/README.md) for the approval authority matrix

### Market Entry
- We only enter markets where we can build a data, operational, or structural advantage within 24 months
- We do not enter markets to "learn" — we learn first, then enter
- Market entry decisions are documented in [strategy/README.md](strategy/README.md)

### Risk Management
- We identify the three most critical risks to every business unit and track them explicitly
- Operational risks are mitigated through process; strategic risks are mitigated through diversification and optionality
- We do not take existential risks for marginal gains

### Partnerships
- We partner to access capabilities we cannot build faster internally
- Partnerships require clear value exchange, defined scope, and exit conditions
- See [partners/README.md](partners/README.md) for partner governance

## Operational Principles

### Documentation First
Before a process scales, it must be documented. Undocumented processes are single points of failure. Every process that runs more than three times needs a written SOP.

See [operations/README.md](operations/README.md) for SOP standards.

### Data Ownership
Every data asset DIS Group generates is a strategic asset. We control our data, we understand it, and we use it. We do not gift data to third parties without explicit strategic justification.

### Technology Choices
We choose technology for long-term maintainability, not trend-following. The approved stack is documented in [technology/stack.md](technology/README.md). Deviations require an Architecture Decision Record (ADR).

### AI Deployment
We deploy AI where it creates measurable advantage — not to signal modernity. Every production AI deployment has a clear purpose, a governance review, and a defined feedback loop. See [ai/governance.md](ai/README.md).

## People Principles

### Hire for Judgment
We hire people who make good decisions with incomplete information. Skills can be taught; judgment is rare. We optimize for cognitive quality, curiosity, and integrity.

### Ownership Culture
Every person at DIS Group is expected to behave like an owner — to notice problems, propose solutions, and take responsibility for outcomes within their domain. Passivity is not rewarded.

### Direct Communication
We say what we mean. Feedback is delivered clearly and respectfully. Problems are surfaced early. We do not use meetings to avoid decisions or documents to avoid conversations.

### Develop People
We invest in developing the people who work here. The company grows when its people grow. Training, mentoring, and expanding responsibility are how we retain the best talent.

## ESG Principles

### Environmental Responsibility
We measure our environmental impact. We set targets to reduce it. We report accurately. We do not offset our way out of problems we should engineer solutions for.

See [esg/README.md](esg/README.md) for the full ESG framework.

### Social Accountability
We take responsibility for how our operations affect the communities and supply chains we touch. Labor standards, community engagement, and supply chain ethics are non-negotiable.

### Governance Integrity
Board decisions are documented. Conflicts of interest are declared. Audit processes are independent. Governance is not a compliance exercise — it is how trust is built.

## Decision-Making Framework

When facing a significant decision, apply this framework:

| Step | Question |
|---|---|
| 1. Clarify | What exactly are we deciding? What are the options? |
| 2. Principles | Does any option violate our principles? Eliminate those. |
| 3. Evidence | What data do we have? What assumptions are we making? |
| 4. Long-term | What does each option look like in 3 years? In 5? |
| 5. Reversibility | Is this reversible? If not, slow down and stress-test. |
| 6. Decide | Make the decision. Document it. Assign an owner. |
| 7. Review | Define a date to review the decision against outcomes. |

Significant decisions (above the thresholds in [finance/approvals.md](finance/README.md)) are documented as decision memos in the relevant domain folder.

## What Happens When Principles Conflict

Principles occasionally conflict. When they do, resolve using this hierarchy:

1. **Integrity** — Always overrides everything else
2. **Long-term value** — Overrides short-term performance
3. **Systems** — Overrides individual convenience
4. **Simplicity** — Overrides completeness when both cannot be achieved

When a genuine conflict arises that cannot be resolved by this hierarchy, escalate to the CEO and document the decision.
