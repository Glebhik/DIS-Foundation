## Slide 3 — The Problem

# Corporate Travel Has a Missing Layer

---

### How Corporate Travel Works Today

1. A company sets a travel policy
2. Travellers search and book — often outside policy
3. Finance reconciles expenses after the fact
4. The carbon impact is calculated months later, if at all
5. No one knows whether the travel was truly necessary, optimally timed, or efficiently managed

---

### The Gap

Corporate travel is a significant cost for most organisations — and it is almost entirely unanalysed in real time.

| What Exists | What Is Missing |
|---|---|
| Booking platforms | Intelligence at the time of decision |
| Expense management | Predictive policy compliance |
| Travel management companies | Carbon intelligence integrated with ESG reporting |
| Static travel policies | Dynamic market context |
| Post-trip reporting | Pre-trip and real-time analytics |

---

### The Opportunity

Not a better booking tool. A fundamentally different layer:

**Travel intelligence as a strategic business function, not an administrative one.**

## Slide 7 — The ESG Angle

# Why the Carbon Intelligence Layer Matters

---

### The Corporate ESG Obligation

Scope 3 emissions — which include business travel — are now material for organisations reporting under GRI, SASB, TCFD, or CSRD. For most companies, business travel represents a significant and historically unmeasured portion of their Scope 3 footprint.

---

### The Current State

| Challenge | Current Reality |
|---|---|
| Carbon measurement | Calculated annually from expense data, retrospectively |
| Attribution | Route-level carbon data is rarely available at booking time |
| Integration | Travel carbon data is siloed from ESG reporting systems |
| Accuracy | Industry averages used in absence of route-specific data |

---

### What DIS Travel Changes

- **At booking time:** Carbon cost is visible alongside financial cost
- **By trip:** Real-time route-level carbon attribution using actual aircraft and route data
- **For reporting:** Carbon data flows directly into organisational ESG reporting infrastructure
- **For decisions:** "Is this trip worth the carbon cost?" becomes an answerable question

---

### The Skyscanner Connection

Route-level flight data — including aircraft type, routing, and schedule — is the raw material for accurate carbon calculation. Skyscanner holds the most comprehensive version of this data globally.

A partnership that includes structured access to route and carrier data is the enabler of DIS Travel's carbon intelligence capability at real-world accuracy.

## Slide 8 — Market Context

# The Moment for Travel Intelligence

---

### Three Converging Forces

**1. AI Capability Has Reached Usable Maturity**
Large language models, structured data pipelines, and AI inference infrastructure are now commercially available at the cost and latency levels required for enterprise software. The intelligence we are building was not technically feasible four years ago at viable economics.

**2. ESG Compliance is Creating New Demand**
The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD), which affects thousands of companies operating in Europe, mandates Scope 3 reporting for in-scope organisations. Business travel is a primary Scope 3 category. The compliance need is creating budget and urgency.

**3. Corporate Travel is Recovering and Restructuring**
Post-2020, corporate travel is returning to volume — but with new stakeholder questions: Is this trip necessary? What is the carbon cost? Can we measure it? The organisational muscle to answer these questions does not yet exist in most companies.

---

### The Window

Early entrants in the travel intelligence category — those who establish architecture, partnerships, and data assets before the market consolidates — will define the category. This is the window DIS Travel is designed to capture.

## Slide 10 — Partnership Vision

# What We Are Proposing to Build Together

---

### The Long-Term Partnership Structure

We are not approaching Skyscanner for a standard API integration. We are proposing a strategic partnership with three components:

---

**Component 1: Data Foundation Partnership**

Structured access to Skyscanner's travel content as the foundation layer of DIS Travel's intelligence. This includes:
- Comprehensive route and carrier data
- Real-time pricing and availability signals
- Historical pricing patterns for market intelligence

---

**Component 2: Distribution Alignment**

DIS Travel as an enterprise channel for Skyscanner's ecosystem. When a DIS Travel corporate user identifies the optimal travel option, the booking path connects back to Skyscanner's supplier network. Enterprise-sourced bookings represent high-intent, high-value transactions.

---

**Component 3: ESG Data Collaboration**

Co-development of route-level carbon intelligence, anchored in Skyscanner's route and carrier data, validated against DIS Group's ESG methodology. This creates a carbon intelligence product neither company could build as accurately alone.

---
