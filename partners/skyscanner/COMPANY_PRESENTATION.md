## Slide 3 — The Problem

# Corporate Travel Has a Missing Layer

---

### How Corporate Travel Decisions Are Made Today

1. A company sets a travel policy — typically a static document
2. Travellers search and book — often on consumer tools, frequently outside policy
3. Finance reconciles expenses after the fact — by which point the spend has already occurred
4. Scope 3 carbon impact is estimated annually from expense data, using industry-average emissions factors
5. No one has a real-time view of whether the programme is compliant, efficient, or aligned with ESG commitments

---

### The Gap — What AI Makes Possible Now

The tools that exist today were built for the previous decade. What has changed is not the problem — it is what is now technically achievable:

| What Has Always Been True | What AI Changes |
|---|---|
| Policy compliance is enforced after the fact | Policy compliance can be assessed at the time of search |
| Carbon attribution requires annual estimates | Route-level carbon can be calculated per trip, in real time |
| Pricing context is unavailable at booking time | Historical and forward pricing patterns can be surfaced at decision time |
| Destination risk is checked manually | AI synthesises risk, visa, and advisory data at the moment it is needed |

The opportunity is not to build a better booking tool. It is to build the intelligence infrastructure that makes every corporate travel decision better than the one before it.

## Slide 7 — The Regulatory Tailwind

# CSRD Is Creating Urgency — Specifically for Travel

---

### The EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

The CSRD, phased in from 2024, mandates Scope 3 emissions reporting for large and mid-sized companies operating in the EU. Business travel falls under Scope 3 Category 6. For the first time, it is not sufficient to estimate travel emissions using industry averages — organisations must demonstrate a methodology that is audit-defensible.

The directive affects tens of thousands of companies in Europe. Most of them do not currently have the data infrastructure to comply accurately.

---

### The Current State of Scope 3 Travel Reporting

| Challenge | Current Reality |
|---|---|
| Measurement timing | Calculated retrospectively from annual expense exports |
| Methodology | Industry average emissions factors applied to spend categories |
| Granularity | Rarely route-level; almost never aircraft-type specific |
| Audit readiness | Typically estimated, not measured — difficult to defend under scrutiny |
| System integration | Travel carbon data is siloed from organisational ESG reporting |

---

### What DIS Travel + Skyscanner Data Changes

- **Per-trip attribution:** Carbon calculated at route level, using actual carrier and aircraft type where available, at booking time
- **Methodology transparency:** ICAO / GHG Protocol compliant — the standard auditors and CSRD expect
- **System integration:** Carbon output flows directly into organisational Scope 3 reporting, not into a separate sustainability dashboard
- **Audit trail:** Every carbon calculation logged with inputs, methodology version, and output

This accuracy requires route-level aircraft-type data. That data relationship is what we are proposing to build with Skyscanner.

## Slide 10 — Partnership Vision

# What We Are Proposing to Build Together

---

### Three Partnership Components

**Component 1: Data Foundation Partnership**

Structured access to Skyscanner's travel content — specifically route, carrier, scheduling, and aircraft metadata — as the data foundation layer for DIS Travel's intelligence. This includes:

- Route and schedule data (origin-destination pairs, carriers, flight frequencies)
- Real-time pricing and availability
- Aircraft type per scheduled route (critical for carbon methodology)
- Historical fare patterns (for market intelligence modelling)

---

**Component 2: Distribution Alignment**

When DIS Travel intelligence drives a booking decision, the booking path connects to Skyscanner's supplier network. Corporate bookings sourced through intelligence tools represent high-intent, context-rich transactions — a different quality than consumer metasearch traffic.

The commercial model for this component is to be designed collaboratively. We are not proposing a specific structure — we are proposing that it be designed based on what creates genuine value for both parties.

---

**Component 3: Carbon Data Collaboration**

Co-development of route-level carbon intelligence anchored in Skyscanner's route and aircraft data, validated against ICAO/GHG Protocol methodology. This creates an output that neither organisation can build at the same accuracy level independently — and that addresses a specific, compliance-driven market need.
