## Executive Summary

DIS Group is an Irish technology company building intelligent businesses at the intersection of physical industrial operations and AI-native software systems. The company operates three active businesses — in recycling operations, energy trading intelligence, and ESG data infrastructure — and is developing DIS Travel, a B2B Travel Intelligence Platform for enterprise corporate travel management.

DIS Group approaches enterprise partnerships before commercial launch by design. The company's view is that data partnerships for an intelligence product are architectural decisions, not commercial afterthoughts. The right partner data must be incorporated into the architecture before the product is built — not integrated into it after.

This document provides enterprise partners with the context required to understand who DIS Group is, how it makes decisions, and why it operates the way it does.

All three operating businesses share a common technology architecture, AI governance framework, and operating principles. This is an operating group with unified intelligence infrastructure — not a holding company with independent subsidiaries.

### DIS Travel — The Fourth Domain

DIS Travel is a B2B Travel Intelligence Platform. It is not a booking platform and does not compete with booking systems. It is the intelligence layer that sits above an organisation's existing booking infrastructure and provides five distinct capabilities:

| Capability | Function |
|---|---|
| **Policy Intelligence** | Analyses booking options against organisational travel policies in real time; classifies compliance, flags exceptions with reasoning |
| **Destination Intelligence** | AI-synthesised briefings on visa requirements, health advisories, security assessments, and local context — per destination, maintained to defined refresh cadence |
| **Carbon and ESG Tracking** | Route-level Scope 3 carbon attribution using ICAO methodology and GHG Protocol; structured output for CSRD audit-ready reporting |
| **Market Intelligence** | Pricing pattern analysis, optimal booking window identification, carrier performance metrics |
| **Supplier Analytics** | Supplier performance tracking and programme-level spend analytics |

**Development status:** Architecture complete. Foundation infrastructure in progress (Q3 2026). Enterprise pilot readiness: Q2–Q3 2027.

## Operating Principles

The principles below govern how DIS Group operates. They are drawn directly from the company's published governance documentation. They are behavioral constraints — when a decision conflicts with a principle, the principle governs the outcome.

**1. Intelligence Before Scale**
DIS Group does not grow until it understands. Before expanding any operation, the company requires clear data on what is working and why. This principle determines when DIS Travel progresses from architecture to build to pilot — not a timeline preference, but a readiness threshold.

**2. Integrity Without Exception**
DIS Group does not optimise short-term optics at the expense of long-term substance. ESG commitments are binding, not marketing. Development status is reported accurately. Partnership conversations do not include capabilities that are not built. This is enforced through documented governance, not enforced through culture statements.

**3. Systems Over Individuals**
DIS Group is built to outlast any individual. Knowledge is documented. Decisions are recorded. Processes are written. The organisation's intelligence lives in its systems. This is the architecture behind the DIS Foundation corporate knowledge system — and the reason DIS Travel's governance, architecture, and compliance design are published documents rather than proprietary internal memos.

**4. Long-Term Thinking**
Capital allocation, product decisions, and partnerships are evaluated on 3–5 year horizons. Short-term indicators are monitored; they are not objectives.

**5. Simplicity as Discipline**
Complexity is a cost. DIS Group prefers simple structures, clear processes, and direct communication. When a system or process is hard to explain, the response is to simplify it — not to add process around it.

**6. Governance Before Growth**
No AI system touches production data, external parties, or material business decisions without completing a Pre-Deployment Review under DIS Group's Class 1–4 risk framework. No commercial commitment is made before the architecture can support it. The governance framework was built before the product — not during it.

## Technology Vision

DIS Group's technology philosophy is that durable competitive advantage in information-intensive markets requires owning the intelligence layer — not merely connecting to data sources. This means building proprietary intelligence systems on top of high-quality external data infrastructure, and making deliberate choices about where to build, where to buy, and where to partner — governed by Architecture Decision Records rather than default vendor relationships.

### AI Governance

DIS Group's AI governance policy is operational today. It classifies AI systems by risk level (Class 1 — Low Risk through Class 4 — Critical), requires Pre-Deployment Review for all Class 2 and above, mandates immutable audit logging for Class 2+ operations, and prohibits passing personal data to external AI models without explicit consent and legal review. The policy was published before any AI system was deployed externally.

For DIS Travel specifically: travel intelligence outputs that inform purchasing decisions are Class 2 (Pre-Deployment Review + quarterly audit); outputs that trigger automated actions are Class 3 (continuous monitoring required). These classifications are established in the architecture design, not post-deployment.

### GDPR by Design

DIS Group is incorporated in Ireland and operates under EU law. GDPR compliance is a data model constraint. Traveller PII is absent from DIS Travel's intelligence layer because the data model does not collect it there — data minimisation and purpose limitation are enforced at the schema level. EU data residency applies at the infrastructure level: cloud provider selection (in progress via ADR process) is constrained to providers capable of guaranteeing EU data residency.

### Architecture Decision Records

Major technology decisions at DIS Group are governed by Architecture Decision Records: documented records of decision drivers, options evaluated, and rationale before committing to a direction. Cloud infrastructure, data platform selection, and integration architecture are all ADR-governed processes. This is how DIS Group has operated from its first technical decision — not a process added for external credibility.

## Partnership Philosophy

DIS Group partners to access capabilities it cannot build faster or better internally. This is a documented operating principle — the Build/Buy/Partner framework is applied to every material capability decision, and the decision rationale is recorded in an ADR.

### Why DIS Travel Pursues Data Partnerships Before Launch

A travel intelligence platform without comprehensive route coverage, real-time pricing data, and carrier metadata is not a travel intelligence platform. These capabilities require established data infrastructure that DIS Group cannot build to the required depth or quality on the timeline that CSRD compliance demands from enterprise customers. The decision to partner rather than build is architectural — documented and deliberate.

Specifically: DIS Travel's ICAO-methodology carbon calculation requires aircraft-type data at route level. This is not available from consumer-facing data sources at the granularity that audit-defensible Scope 3 reporting requires. A structured data relationship with the right partner is an architectural necessity.

### What DIS Group Brings to a Data Partnership

- **Long-term commitment:** 3–5 year horizon. DIS Group does not enter partnerships for short-term commercial terms and does not exit them when the immediate objective is met.
- **Technical readiness:** Integration architecture is documented before commercial negotiation begins. DIS Travel's Technical Overview provides a three-layer integration design for Skyscanner-class partnerships: Content Foundation, Booking Distribution, and Carbon Data Enrichment.
- **EU compliance:** GDPR-by-design architecture, EU data residency at infrastructure level, DPA template available for review. Partner data is processed within EU infrastructure. No personal traveller data is transmitted to partner APIs.
- **Governance transparency:** Published AI governance policy, Architecture Decision Records, honest development status. Partners are not asked to take governance on faith.
- **Non-competitive positioning:** DIS Travel serves the organisations that employ travellers. It does not compete with platforms that serve travellers directly. The relationship is complementary by architecture.

## Why Enterprise Partners Work With DIS Group

Enterprise data partners assess DIS Group against the same criteria they apply to any long-term data relationship: Does this company protect data correctly? Will it honour commitments? Is the technical integration sound? Will it still be operating in three years?

The following addresses each question from documented, verifiable evidence only.

**On data protection:** GDPR compliance is designed at the data model level — traveller PII does not enter the intelligence layer because the schema does not collect it there. EU data residency is an infrastructure-level constraint, not a contractual promise contingent on vendor compliance. These are architectural facts.

**On commitment:** DIS Group operates three active businesses in capital-intensive, regulated markets — recycling, energy, and ESG data. These are not easily exited positions. The company's documented operating principle is to evaluate decisions on a 3–5 year horizon. DIS Travel was designed with this same horizon.

**On technical soundness:** DIS Travel's architecture is documented to the level of data domain models, five-layer system design, integration layer specifications, security controls, and explicit methodology citations (ICAO, GHG Protocol Scope 3 Category 6). The Technical Overview includes honest development status labels and specific technical questions that demonstrate architectural understanding of partner systems — not general interest.

**On governance:** DIS Group published its AI governance policy, GDPR compliance framework, and Architecture Decision Record process before building DIS Travel, not in response to a partnership request. Governance-first is the operating order, not a positioning claim.

**On transparency:** DIS Group communicates development status accurately and does not represent designed components as implemented ones. The development status table above uses the word "pending" where implementation has not yet started. Enterprise partnerships built on accurate mutual understanding are more durable than those built on optimistic representation.
